# Part 1 — The Offer

## Business Model

You find small businesses with no website, call them cold, and offer to build a professional website for a flat build fee plus a mandatory 12-month Ongoing Care Plan at $79/month. The key insight: **there are hundreds of thousands of businesses in any metro area without a website.** They're not hard to find — they just haven't been asked yet.

**Target customers (high need, fast decision-makers):**
- Restaurants, diners, cafés
- Plumbers, electricians, HVAC, general contractors
- Hair salons, barbershops, nail salons
- Auto repair shops
- Landscapers, lawn care
- Cleaning services
- Florists, gift shops
- Local law offices, accountants
- Chiropractors, massage therapists

**Avoid (for now):**
- Large chains (have corporate web teams)
- Businesses with existing websites — even bad ones (different pitch entirely)
- Startups (different needs, slower buying cycle)

## Ongoing Care Plan — $79/month, 12-month minimum

**Required with every build. Not optional. 12-month minimum term.** After 12 months it converts to month-to-month; client may cancel with 30 days' written notice.

| Deliverable | Details |
|---|---|
| Hosting | Vercel — fast global CDN, free SSL |
| Security updates | SSL renewal, dependency patches, monitoring |
| Website edits | Text changes, new photos, updated hours, seasonal promos. Budget ~1 hr/month per client. |
| Backup / maintenance | Site files versioned in GitHub; restorable any time |
| Support | Same-day weekday response by email |

**Term + billing:**
- Months 1–12 are mandatory. Cancellation during this period requires payment of remaining months in full.
- First month ($79) collected at launch alongside the build balance.
- Months 2–12 billed automatically each month on the launch-day anniversary.
- **First-year client commitment:** $948 (12 × $79).

**Why mandatory?** Lets you offer a low one-time build fee. Guarantees the site stays live and current. Eliminates the "what now?" moment after launch. Creates predictable recurring revenue.

## Pricing Psychology & Objections

**One package keeps the pitch simple.** No tier confusion, no "wait, what's the difference" objections. You say what it is, you say what it costs, you ask for the deposit.

**Why $599 and not $500.** Round numbers feel arbitrary. $599 feels considered. It's still far cheaper than the $2,500–$10,000 a local agency would quote — that's your anchor.

**Why disclose the 12-month commitment up front.** Disclosing the full $948 on the first call kills resistance later. Anchor on the **first-year total of $1,547** — it's the real number, and it's still cheaper than one month of a marketing agency retainer.

**The $79/month framing:**
> *"Think of it like a phone plan for your website — it's running, maintained, and you have someone to call. Most of my clients use the included edits every month."*

### Handling price objections

**"That's too expensive."**
> "I totally understand. Just for context — a local agency would charge $2,500–$10,000 for the same scope, with monthly retainers of $500+. We're well under that, and you get everything bundled — build, hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support for a year."

**"I can get a website for $10/month from Wix."**
> "Absolutely — Wix is a great tool if you have 20 hours to learn it and design it yourself. What I offer is done-for-you: I handle the design, the copy, the setup, the hosting, the Google listing — and I keep the site current every month. You don't touch anything."

**"My nephew can do it."**
> "Awesome — use him! If it doesn't work out or takes too long, I'm always here. I can turn one around in a week."

**"Why is the $79/month required for 12 months?"**
> "Two reasons. One, it's how I can offer the build at $599 instead of $1,500. Two, it covers hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support — the things that keep your site actually doing its job long after launch. After the first year it's month-to-month and you can cancel anytime."

**"Can I just pay for the website and host it myself?"**
> "We don't unbundle the build from the Care Plan — every site we ship comes with the 12-month plan. That's how we keep quality high and the upfront cost low. If you want to move hosting elsewhere after the 12 months, no problem — your site files are yours."

**"What if I want to cancel during the 12 months?"**
> "The 12-month term is fixed, just like a phone contract. If you ever needed to end it early, the remaining months would be due. After month 12 you can cancel anytime with 30 days' notice."

# Part 2 — The Client Journey

```
LEAD GEN → COLD CALL → DISCOVERY → PROPOSAL → DEPOSIT → BUILD → REVIEW → LAUNCH → CARE PLAN
```

## 1. Lead Generation

**Tool:** `scraper/find_leads.py`
**Output:** CSV with business name, phone, address, Google Maps link.

1. Run scraper every morning with 2–3 business types + your target city
2. Open the CSV in Excel or Google Sheets
3. Sort by rating (descending) — higher-rated businesses lose the most without a web presence
4. Remove duplicates from previous days
5. Add new leads to your master tracking sheet

**Master tracking sheet columns:** Business Name | Phone | Address | Type | Date Added | Called? | Outcome | Follow-up

**Goal:** 20+ new leads per day. ~100 calls per closed client.

---

## 2. Cold Calling

**Target:** 20 calls/day minimum.
**Best hours:** Tue–Thu, 9–11am and 2–4pm.
**Tone:** Friendly, confident, not salesy. You're doing them a favor by pointing out an easy win.
**Goal of the call:** Book a 15-minute discovery call. That's it. Don't pitch yet.

### Call outcomes to track
- `VM` — Left voicemail (follow up in 2 days)
- `NA` — No answer (try again next day, different time)
- `NI` — Not interested (mark closed, do not call again)
- `CB` — Callback requested (note date/time)
- `INT` — Interested, discovery call booked
- `CLOSED` — Agreed to proceed

### The opening (first 10 seconds matter most)

> "Hi, is this [Name/Business Name]?"
> *(wait for yes)*
>
> "Hey [Name], this is Brian calling from Frontpage Studio. I'm a local web designer and I was actually just looking up [type of business] in [city] — I noticed your business doesn't have a website yet. Is that right?"

*(Wait for response — most will say "yeah" or "not yet" or "we've been meaning to")*

### If they confirm (no website)

> "Totally, a lot of great businesses haven't gotten around to it yet. I actually build websites specifically for [restaurants/plumbers/salons/etc.] — I can have something up and running in about a week, and it's a flat rate so there's no surprises."
>
> "Would you be open to a quick 10-minute call — just so I can understand your business a little and show you what one could look like for you? Totally free, no obligation."

### Handling common responses

**"We're not interested."**
> "Completely fair — can I ask, is it more of a budget thing or just not a priority right now?"
*(If budget): "Just so you know, my package is $599 one-time plus $79/month — required for the first 12 months, then month-to-month — and that covers hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support. Most local agencies are 3–5x that. Want me to send over a quick example?"*
*(If not priority): "No problem at all. Mind if I send you an email just in case? I'll include a demo site so you can see what I build."*

**"We already have someone working on it."**
> "Oh perfect, good for you! If it ever doesn't work out, keep me in mind — I do flat-rate, one-week turnarounds. Good luck with it!"
*(Move on)*

**"How much does it cost?"**
> "I keep it simple — one package: $599 to build your site, then $79/month for hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support. The monthly plan runs for the first 12 months and then becomes month-to-month. First-year total works out to about $1,547. Does that sound reasonable for what you'd be looking for?"

**"I have a Facebook page, that's enough."**
> "That's a great start — and a lot of customers will find you there. The difference with a website is Google. When someone types '[plumber/restaurant/etc.] near me' into Google, they see websites first — Facebook pages rank much lower. So you're probably not showing up for new customers who don't know you yet. That's the gap a website fills."

**"Send me something in the mail / by email."**
> "Sure, what's the best email? I'll send you a quick example of a site I've built for a similar business — takes 30 seconds to look at. And if you like what you see, we can set up that quick call."
*(Get the email — this is a warm lead now)*

**"Call me back / I'm busy right now."**
> "Of course — what's a better time? I can call Tuesday morning, or Thursday afternoon — which works better?"
*(Get a specific time. Vague callbacks don't happen.)*

**"I can't afford it right now."**
> "I totally understand — when would be a better time to revisit this? I'm not going anywhere. Even 3 months from now, I'd love to help you get online."
*(Note the callback date)*

**"Why do I have to commit to 12 months of the $79?"**
> "Two reasons. One — it's how I can offer the build at $599 instead of $1,500 like the agencies charge. Two — it covers your hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support for the whole first year. After 12 months it's month-to-month and you can cancel anytime. Most clients stay because it's easier than moving."

### Booking the discovery call

> "Perfect. It's just a quick 15-minute phone call. What works better for you, tomorrow morning around 10, or Thursday afternoon around 2?"

*(Lock in a specific day and time on the spot. Confirm by text or email right after the call so they have it written down.)*

> "Great, [Day] at [Time] it is. I'll send a quick confirmation text right after we hang up. Looking forward to it, [Name]!"

### Voicemail script

> "Hey [Name], this is Brian with Frontpage Studio — I'm a web designer and I noticed [Business] doesn't have a website yet. I'd love to show you what one could look like — it's a quick call, totally free. My number is [your number] — feel free to call or text me back. Thanks!"

**Voicemail length:** Under 20 seconds. Short = more callbacks.

### Cold-call tips

1. **Call in blocks.** Don't mix calling with building. Dedicate 2 hours morning, 1 hour afternoon. Switch modes.
2. **Don't pitch on the first call.** The goal is the discovery call. The close happens on call 2.
3. **Don't apologize for calling.** You have something valuable. Act like it.
4. **Use their name.** Once at the start, once at the end.
5. **Smile while you talk.** It comes through in your voice.
6. **Stand up while calling.** More energy, more confidence.
7. **Track everything.** Date | Business | Outcome at minimum.
8. **80 calls = 1 client.** That's the math. Stay consistent.

## 3. Discovery Call

**Duration:** 15–20 minutes.
**Goal:** Qualify the prospect, understand their needs, set up for proposal.

### Opening
> "Hi [Name], Brian from Frontpage Studio — thanks for making the time! I'll keep this short, I just have a few quick questions so I can put together the right proposal for you. Sound good?"

### Key questions
1. "Tell me a little about [Business] — how long have you been open?"
2. "How do most of your customers find you right now — mostly word of mouth, Yelp, Google?"
3. "What do you most want a website to do for you — just be a presence online, or are you looking to get new customers from Google?"
4. "Do you already have a logo and brand colors, or would you need help with that too?"
5. "Any websites you've seen for similar businesses that you like the look of?"
6. "What's the best way for customers to contact you — phone, email, or form?"

### Qualify on
- Budget? ($599 build + $79/month × 12 = $1,547 first year)
- Decision-maker?
- Urgency? (If yes, move fast)

### Close
> "Perfect — based on everything you've told me, the Business Presence Package will be a great fit. I'm going to put together a quick proposal and send it over tonight. It'll include exactly what's included and the price. Does [tomorrow morning] work to follow up?"

## 5. Deposit & Agreement

**Tools:** Stripe (set up in Appendix A) + `templates/msa.html`.

**For your first ~10 clients:** a "Yes, let's do it!" email reply combined with payment of the deposit constitutes acceptance of the attached proposal. For B2B services at this price point, this is legally sufficient and is what most freelancers use.

**For more deliberate engagements (recommended once you're past first few clients):** attach the Master Service Agreement (`templates/msa.html` → print to PDF) alongside the proposal. Both parties sign before the deposit is paid. The MSA covers scope, payment, the 12-month Care Plan term, domain pass-through, refunds, IP ownership, and governing law. It references `templates/refund-policy.md` for the refund schedule by stage.

**After the deposit clears:** send the welcome packet (`templates/welcome-packet.html` → print to PDF, attach to the deposit confirmation email). It orients the client on what they paid for, the 7-day timeline, what you need from them, and how the Care Plan works after launch. Email template is in Part 3 §5.

**Payment terms:**
- 50% deposit ($299.50) before build starts
- 50% balance ($299.50) + first month Care Plan ($79) = $378.50 at launch
- Plus domain pass-through (~$12 to $15) at launch if Brian is registering one for the client. Re-billed at cost annually.
- Then $79/month auto-charged for 11 more months

**Never start building without a deposit.** Even with nice people. One ghost will teach you this lesson.

### Day 3–5: Build

### Day 7: Client review
Send the staging link (Vercel preview URL). Use the staging-review email in [Part 3 §7](#7-staging-review).

## 9. Care Plan Ongoing

Every client signs up for the $79/month Care Plan at launch for a **minimum of 12 months**. Non-negotiable — it's how Frontpage Studio offers a low one-time build fee while keeping every site running.

### How to handle it
- Collect the first month's $79 with the final build payment at launch
- Stripe subscription auto-charges $79 on the launch-date anniversary each month for the remaining 11 months
- Log every active client in your tracking spreadsheet with start date and month-12 anniversary

### Monthly cadence
- **1st of every month:** quick security check across all client sites (incognito visit to confirm padlock, Vercel dashboard for failed deploys, Formspree spam patterns). See `BUILD-GUIDE.md` §15 for the checklist.
- **Ongoing:** batch website edits weekly. Budget ~1 hour/month per client.
- **Quarterly:** Lighthouse audit + external link check + domain expiry check per client. See `BUILD-GUIDE.md` §17.

### Month-11 email
Send a courtesy email at month 11 confirming the initial term wraps up next month and hosting continues uninterrupted. Template is in `BUILD-GUIDE.md` §19.

### Cancellations
- **During months 1–12:** politely remind them of the agreement; remaining months are due in full.
- **After month 12:** hand over files within 24 hours; point their domain wherever they want; done.

### If a client routinely exceeds 1 hr/month of edits
Email them a usage summary at any point and offer either: bill overage at $75/hr, or upgrade to a 2 hr/month plan at $129/month going forward.

# Appendix A — One-Time Setup

Do this once before your first client call. Most takes a few hours total, plus 1–2 days waiting for verifications.

## A1. Domain (~$12/yr)
1. Go to **namecheap.com** or **cloudflare.com/registrar** (Cloudflare is at-cost, cheapest)
2. Search `frontpagestudio.com` (or `frontpage.studio` if taken)
3. Buy it

## A2. Company Email (Zoho Mail, free)
1. Go to **zoho.com/mail** → "Get Started for Free" → "Forever Free Plan"
2. Enter your domain
3. Verify ownership by adding a TXT DNS record at your registrar
4. Create inbox `brian@frontpagestudio.com`
5. Install Zoho Mail app on your phone
6. Test: send yourself an email from Gmail, confirm it arrives

## A3. GitHub
1. Sign up at **github.com** (free)
2. Create a repo called `frontpagestudio`
3. Upload the contents of your local `web/` folder

## A4. Vercel (free hosting)
1. Sign up at **vercel.com** with your GitHub account
2. **Add New Project** → import the `frontpagestudio` repo
3. Default settings → **Deploy**
4. You get a free URL like `frontpagestudio.vercel.app` immediately
5. Add your custom domain: Settings → Domains → add `frontpagestudio.com`, follow CNAME instructions at your registrar

## A5. Formspree (contact form, free)
1. Sign up at **formspree.io**
2. **New Form** → name it "Frontpage Studio Contact"
3. Copy the form endpoint URL (looks like `https://formspree.io/f/XXXXXXXX`)
4. Edit `index.html` — find `action="https://formspree.io/f/YOUR_FORM_ID"` and replace `YOUR_FORM_ID` with your actual ID
5. Push to GitHub — Vercel auto-redeploys
6. Test by submitting the form

## A6. Stripe (payments)
1. Sign up at **stripe.com** → start application
2. Enter business info (sole proprietor is fine)
3. Connect your bank account
4. Wait for verification (hours to a couple of days)
5. Dashboard → Products → create:
   - **Business Presence Package** (one-time, $599)
   - **Ongoing Care Plan** (recurring, $79/month)
6. Dashboard → Payment Links → generate three links you reuse for every client:
   - **Deposit link:** $299.50 one-time
   - **Launch link:** $378.50 one-time
   - **Subscription link:** $79/month recurring
7. Save the three URLs (notes app or password manager)
8. **Then:** wire them into `templates/proposal.html` (`[STRIPE_DEPOSIT_LINK]`), `templates/invoice.html` (`[STRIPE_LAUNCH_LINK]`), and the email templates in Part 3

**Fees:** Card 2.9% + $0.30 (~$48/client/year on $1,547 revenue). ACH 1.5% capped at $5 — offer for the big build payment.

## A7. Client Intake Google Form (~10 min)
1. **forms.google.com** → blank form → name it "Frontpage Studio — New Client Intake"
2. Open `templates/client-intake.md` and copy each question into the form
3. For Logo and Photos sections, use **File upload** question type. Allow image + PDF. Cap at 10MB. Allow multiple files for photos.
4. Settings → Responses → enable **Collect email addresses** + **Link to Google Sheet**
5. **Send** → copy the form link → save it next to your Stripe links

## A8. Google Places API (for the scraper)
1. Go to **console.cloud.google.com**
2. Create a project named "Frontpage Studio Leads"
3. Enable **Places API (New)**
4. Credentials → Create Credentials → API Key
5. On your laptop: `pip install -r scraper/requirements.txt`
6. Set the env var: `$env:GOOGLE_PLACES_API_KEY = "your-key"` (PowerShell) or put it in a `.env` file in `scraper/`
7. Run: `python scraper/find_leads.py`

## A9. Things Claude cannot do for you

These require your accounts, payment, or authentication:

1. Register your domain
2. Set up Zoho Mail (verify domain ownership)
3. Create GitHub / Vercel / Formspree / Stripe / Google accounts
4. Get a Google Places API key
5. Set up Google Business Profile (postcard verification)
6. Register an LLC (legalzoom.com or your state's SOS website, ~$50–$150)
7. Take actual payment from clients (need a live payment account)
