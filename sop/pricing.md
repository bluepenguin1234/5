## YOUR OFFER (One Package)

Frontpage Studio sells **one package**, paired with a **mandatory 12-month Ongoing Care Plan**.

**Includes:**
- 3-page professional website (Home, Services, Contact)
- Mobile-friendly design
- Contact form + click-to-call
- Reviews / testimonials section
- Photo gallery
- Business hours + service area displayed

- Search-ready setup (page titles & meta descriptions, GBP connection, consistent NAP, LocalBusiness schema markup)

**Your cost to deliver:** ~3–5 hours to build, ~30–60 min/month ongoing  
**First payment (deposit):** $299.50 (50% of build)  
**Launch-day payment:** $299.50 (balance) + $79 (first month Care Plan) = **$378.50**

### ONGOING CARE PLAN — $79/month (mandatory 12-month minimum)

**Required with every build. Not optional.** The 12-month term is non-negotiable; after 12 months it converts to month-to-month and the client may cancel with 30 days' notice.

**Includes:**
- Hosting (Vercel — costs you $0)
- Security updates + SSL maintenance
- Website edits (text, photos, hours, prices, seasonal banners — budget ~1 hr/month per client)
- Backup / maintenance
- Email support (same-day weekday response)

**First-year commitment per client:** $948 (12 × $79)  
**First-year total per client:** **$1,547** ($599 build + $948 Care Plan)

**Why mandatory?**  
- Lets us offer a low one-time build fee
- Guarantees the site stays live, secure, and current
- Eliminates the "what now?" moment after launch — every client always has support
- Creates predictable recurring revenue for the business

**Pitch script if pushed:**  
> *"The $79/month covers your hosting, security, ongoing edits, backups and maintenance, and support. It's required for the first 12 months — that's how we keep the upfront price so low. After the first year it's month-to-month and you can cancel anytime."*

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

**One package keeps the pitch simple.** No tier confusion, no "wait, what's the difference" objections. You say what it is, you say what it costs, you ask for the deposit.

**Why $599 and not $500:**  
Round numbers feel arbitrary. $599 feels considered. It's still far cheaper than the $2,500–$10,000 a local agency would quote — that's your comparison point.

**Why frame the 12-month Care Plan up front:**  
Disclosing the full 12-month commitment ($948) on the first call kills resistance later. Anchor on the **first-year total of $1,547** in your proposal — it's the real number, and it's still cheaper than one month of a marketing agency retainer.

**The $79/month is easier to sell than you think.** Frame it as:  
> *"Think of it like a phone plan for your website — it's running, maintained, and you have someone to call. Most of my clients use the included edits every month."*  
Most business owners spend more than that on coffee.

## HANDLING PRICE OBJECTIONS

**"That's too expensive."**
> "I totally understand. Just for context — a local agency would charge $2,500–$10,000 for the same scope, with monthly retainers of $500+. We're well under that, and you get everything bundled — build, hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support for a year."

**"I can get a website for $10/month from Wix."**
> "Absolutely — Wix is a great tool if you have 20 hours to learn it and design it yourself. What I offer is done-for-you: I handle the design, the copy, the setup, the hosting, the Google listing — and I keep the site current every month. You don't touch anything."

**"My nephew can do it."**
> "Awesome — use him! If it doesn't work out or takes too long, I'm always here. I can turn one around in a week."

**"Why is the $79/month required for 12 months?"**
> "Two reasons. One, it's how I can offer the build at $599 instead of $1,500. Two, it covers hosting, security, edits, backups and maintenance, and support — the things that keep your site actually doing its job long after launch. After the first year it's month-to-month and you can cancel anytime."

**"Can I just pay for the website and host it myself?"**
> "We don't unbundle the build from the Care Plan — every site we ship comes with the 12-month plan. That's how we keep quality high and the upfront cost low. If you want to move hosting elsewhere after the 12 months, no problem — your site files are yours."

**"What if I want to cancel during the 12 months?"**
> "The 12-month term is fixed, just like a phone contract. If you ever needed to end it early, the remaining months would be due. After month 12 you can cancel anytime with 30 days' notice."
