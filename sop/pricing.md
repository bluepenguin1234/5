## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

**Always lead with Professional ($999).** Most people buy the middle option when there are multiple tiers — but since we only have two, the Professional is your anchor. Drop to Starter only if they push back on price.

**Why $599 and not $500:**
Round numbers feel arbitrary. $599 feels considered. It's still far cheaper than the $2,500–$10,000 a local agency would quote — that's your comparison point.

**The $79/month is easier to sell than you think.** Frame it as: *"Think of it like a phone plan for your website — it's running, maintained, and you have someone to call."* Most business owners spend more than that on coffee.

## HANDLING PRICE OBJECTIONS

**"That's too expensive."**
> "I totally understand. Can I ask — what were you expecting to pay? I want to make sure we find something that works for you."
*(Most haven't thought about it. Educate on value — a local agency charges $3,000–$10,000 for the same thing.)*

**"I can get a website for $10/month from Wix."**
> "Absolutely — Wix is a great tool if you have 20 hours to learn it and design it yourself. What I offer is done-for-you: I handle the design, the copy, the setup, and the hosting. You don't touch anything."

**"My nephew can do it."**
> "Awesome — use him! If it doesn't work out or takes too long, I'm always here. I can turn one around in a week."

**"Why do I have to pay $79 every month?"**
> "It covers your hosting so your site stays live, plus an hour of updates each month — hours, new photos, anything that changes. Most of my clients use it every month. And if you ever want to cancel, I'll hand over all your files."
