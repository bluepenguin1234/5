# Cold Call Script — Frontpage Studio

> **Tone:** Friendly, confident, not salesy. You're doing them a favor by pointing out an easy win.  
> **Speed:** Slow down. Pause after asking questions. Let them talk.  
> **Goal of this call:** Book a 15-minute discovery call. That's it.

---

## THE OPENING (First 10 seconds matter most)

> "Hi, is this [Name/Business Name]?"  
> *(wait for yes)*  
>  
> "Hey [Name], this is Brian calling from Frontpage Studio. I'm a local web designer and I was actually just looking up [type of business] in [city] — I noticed your business doesn't have a website yet. Is that right?"

*(Wait for response — most will say "yeah" or "not yet" or "we've been meaning to")*

---

## IF THEY CONFIRM (No website)

> "Totally, a lot of great businesses haven't gotten around to it yet. I actually build websites specifically for [restaurants/plumbers/salons/etc.] — I can have something up and running in about a week, and it's a flat rate so there's no surprises."  
>  
> "Would you be open to a quick 10-minute call — just so I can understand your business a little and show you what one could look like for you? Totally free, no obligation."

---

## HANDLING COMMON RESPONSES

### "We're not interested."
> "Completely fair — can I ask, is it more of a budget thing or just not a priority right now?"  
*(If budget): "I have a starter option at $599 — one-time fee, no monthly costs. Would that be more in range?"*  
*(If not priority): "No problem at all. Mind if I send you an email just in case? I'll include a demo site so you can see what I build."*

### "We already have someone working on it."
> "Oh perfect, good for you! If it ever doesn't work out, keep me in mind — I do flat-rate, one-week turnarounds. Good luck with it!"  
*(Move on — don't waste time here)*

### "How much does it cost?"
> "It depends on exactly what you need — that's actually why I'd love that quick 10-minute call. But just so you know, most of my clients are in the $599 to $999 range. Does that sound reasonable for what you'd be looking for?"

### "I have a Facebook page, that's enough."
> "That's a great start — and a lot of customers will find you there. The difference with a website is Google. When someone types '[plumber/restaurant/etc.] near me' into Google, they see websites first — Facebook pages rank much lower. So you're probably not showing up for new customers who don't know you yet. That's the gap a website fills."

### "Send me something in the mail / by email."
> "Sure, what's the best email? I'll send you a quick example of a site I've built for a similar business — takes 30 seconds to look at. And if you like what you see, we can set up that quick call."  
*(Get the email address — this is a warm lead now)*

### "Call me back / I'm busy right now."
> "Of course — what's a better time? I can call Tuesday morning, or Thursday afternoon — which works better?"  
*(Get a specific time. Vague callbacks don't happen.)*

### "I can't afford it right now."
> "I totally understand — when would be a better time to revisit this? I'm not going anywhere. Even 3 months from now, I'd love to help you get online."  
*(Note the callback date in your tracker)*

**Voicemail length:** Under 20 seconds. Short = more callbacks.

---

## TIPS FROM THE FIELD

1. **Call in blocks.** Don't mix calling with building. Dedicate 2 hours in the morning, 1 hour in the afternoon — then switch modes.

2. **Don't pitch on the first call.** Your only goal is to get a discovery call booked. The close happens on call 2.

3. **Don't apologize for calling.** You have something valuable. Act like it.

4. **Use their name.** Once at the start, once at the end.

5. **Smile while you talk.** It comes through in your voice.

6. **Stand up while calling.** More energy, more confidence.

7. **Track everything.** If you're not tracking, you can't improve. Even a basic spreadsheet: Date | Business | Outcome.

8. **80 calls = 1 client.** Don't get discouraged. That's the math. 20 calls/day = 1 new client per week.

## DISCOVERY CALL SCRIPT

*(For when you get the 15-min call booked)*

**Opening:**
> "Hi [Name], Brian from Frontpage Studio — thanks for making the time! I'll keep this short, I just have a few quick questions so I can put together the right proposal for you. Sound good?"

**Key questions:**
1. "Tell me a little about [Business] — how long have you been open?"
2. "How do most of your customers find you right now — mostly word of mouth, Yelp, Google?"
3. "What do you most want a website to do for you — just be a presence online, or are you looking to get new customers from Google?"
4. "Do you already have a logo and brand colors, or would you need help with that too?"
5. "Any websites you've seen for similar businesses that you like the look of?"

**Close:**
> "Perfect — based on everything you've told me, I'm going to put together a quick proposal and send it over tonight. It'll include a recommended package, exactly what's included, and the price. Does [tomorrow morning] work to follow up?"
